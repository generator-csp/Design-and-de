Agricultural Solid Waste It’ll blow your mind.

Agricultural solid waste refers to the non-liquid organic and inorganic residues generated from agricultural activities. This includes waste produced during the cultivation of crops , harvesting , processing and animal husbandry. Common examoles include crop residues(straw, husks , stems, leaves) , animal manure, poultry litter, silageplastic, pesticide containers and spoiled grains.

Biogas Production. See for yourself.

Biogas production is a renewable energy process that involves the anaerobic digestion of organic materials, such as agricultural waste, manure, and food scraps, to produce biogas. This biogas can be used as a clean energy source for heating, electricity generation, and transportation.

Recycling. Checkmate.

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. It is an essential part of waste management and helps reduce the consumption of fresh raw materials, decrease energy usage, lower greenhouse gas emissions, and reduce pollution.

Environment Love. See for yourself.

Loving the environment means respecting, protecting and nurturing the natural world that sustains all life.
